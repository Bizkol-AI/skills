# Brand Voice Framework

Complete framework for documenting, applying, and enforcing brand voice across client marketing content.

## Brand Voice Documentation Template

Every client's brand voice should be documented in their `client-brief.md`. Use this framework to help clients define their voice or to review content against existing guidelines.

### 1. Brand Personality

Define the brand as if it were a person. What are its defining traits?

Example: "If our brand were a person, they would be a knowledgeable colleague who explains complex things simply, celebrates your wins genuinely, and never talks down to you."

### 2. Voice Attributes (3-5)

For each attribute, document:

**[Attribute Name]**
- **We are**: what this means in practice
- **We are not**: common misinterpretation to avoid
- **This sounds like**: example sentence demonstrating the attribute
- **This does NOT sound like**: example sentence violating the attribute

#### Common Voice Attribute Spectrums

| Spectrum | One End | Other End |
|----------|---------|-----------|
| Formality | Formal, institutional | Casual, conversational |
| Authority | Expert, authoritative | Peer-level, collaborative |
| Emotion | Warm, empathetic | Direct, matter-of-fact |
| Complexity | Technical, precise | Simple, accessible |
| Energy | Bold, energetic | Calm, measured |
| Humor | Playful, witty | Serious, earnest |
| Innovation | Cutting-edge, forward-looking | Established, proven |

### 3. Audience Awareness

- Who the brand speaks to (primary and secondary audiences)
- What the audience cares about
- Audience expertise level
- How the audience expects to be addressed

### 4. Core Messaging Pillars (3-5)

- Key themes the brand consistently communicates
- Hierarchy of messages (which comes first)
- How each pillar connects to audience needs
- Proof points supporting each pillar

### 5. Tone Spectrum

The voice stays consistent; tone adapts to context. Tone is the emotional inflection applied to the voice.

#### Tone by Channel

| Channel | Tone Adaptation |
|---------|----------------|
| Blog | Informative, conversational, educational |
| Social (LinkedIn) | Professional, thought-provoking, concise |
| Social (Twitter/X) | Punchy, direct, sometimes witty |
| Email marketing | Personal, helpful, action-oriented |
| Sales collateral | Confident, benefit-driven, specific |
| Press release | Formal, factual, newsworthy |

#### Tone by Situation

| Situation | Tone Adaptation |
|-----------|----------------|
| Product launch | Excited, confident, forward-looking |
| Incident or outage | Transparent, empathetic, accountable |
| Customer success story | Celebratory, specific, crediting the customer |
| Thought leadership | Authoritative, nuanced, evidence-based |
| Competitive comparison | Confident but fair, fact-based, not disparaging |

**Tone adaptation rule:** Voice attributes remain fixed. Tone dials them up or down based on context.

### 6. Style Rules

| Rule | Options | Example |
|------|---------|---------|
| Oxford comma | Yes / No | "fast, reliable, and secure" vs. "fast, reliable and secure" |
| Heading case | Sentence / Title | "How to get started" vs. "How to Get Started" |
| Contractions | Use / Avoid | "we're" vs. "we are" |
| Numbers | Spell out 1-9 / Always numerals | "five features" vs. "5 features" |
| Percent | % / percent | "50%" vs. "50 percent" |
| Date format | Month DD, YYYY / DD/MM/YYYY | "January 15, 2025" |
| Exclamation marks | Limited / None | Never more than one |
| Emoji usage | By channel — professional: none; social: where appropriate |

### 7. Terminology Management

#### Preferred Terms Table

| Use This | Not This | Notes |
|----------|----------|-------|
| sign up (verb) | signup (verb) | "signup" is the noun form |
| log in (verb) | login (verb) | "login" is the noun/adjective form |
| email | e-mail | No hyphen |

Document per client:
- Official product/feature name capitalization
- When to use full name vs. shorthand
- Trademark and registration symbol requirements
- Acronyms that must be spelled out on first use
- Competitor terms to avoid (to prevent reinforcing their positioning)

#### Inclusive Language Standards
- Gender-neutral language (they/them for unknown individuals)
- Avoid ableist language ("crazy", "blind spot", "lame")
- Avoid culturally specific idioms that may not translate
- Use "straightforward" instead of "easy" (what is easy varies by person)

## Brand Review Methodology

When reviewing content against brand voice (used by `/brand-review`):

### With Brand Guidelines Configured

Evaluate against each dimension:

1. **Voice and Tone** — Does content match defined voice attributes? Is tone appropriate for the content type? Flag specific sentences that deviate.
2. **Terminology and Language** — Are preferred terms used? Are "avoid" terms present? Is jargon appropriate for the audience?
3. **Messaging Pillars** — Does content align with defined pillars? Are claims consistent with approved messaging?
4. **Style Guide Compliance** — Grammar, punctuation, formatting, numbers, dates per style guide.

### Without Brand Guidelines (Generic Review)

1. **Clarity** — Is the main message clear in the first paragraph? Are sentences concise?
2. **Consistency** — Is tone consistent throughout? Are terms used consistently?
3. **Professionalism** — Free of errors? Tone appropriate for audience? Claims substantiated?

### Legal and Compliance Flags (Always Checked)

Regardless of brand guidelines, flag:
- **Unsubstantiated claims** — superlatives ("best", "fastest", "only") without evidence
- **Missing disclaimers** — financial, health, or guarantee claims that may need legal review
- **Comparative claims** — comparisons to competitors that could be challenged
- **Regulatory language** — content needing compliance review
- **Testimonial issues** — quotes without attribution or disclosure
- **Copyright concerns** — closely paraphrased content from other sources

### Review Output Format

| Issue | Location | Severity | Suggestion |
|-------|----------|----------|------------|

Severity levels:
- **High** — contradicts brand voice, compliance risk, or significantly undermines messaging
- **Medium** — inconsistent with guidelines but not damaging
- **Low** — minor style or preference issue
