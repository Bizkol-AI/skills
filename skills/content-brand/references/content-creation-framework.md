# Content Creation Framework

Templates, best practices, and SEO guidelines for creating marketing content across all major channels.

## Content Type Templates

### Blog Post

1. **Headline** — clear, benefit-driven, includes primary keyword (under 60 characters for SEO)
2. **Introduction** (100-150 words) — hook with a question, statistic, bold claim, or story. State what the post covers. Include primary keyword.
3. **Body sections** (3-5 sections) — descriptive H2 subheadings, one core idea per section with supporting evidence/examples/data. Use H3 for subsections.
4. **Conclusion** (75-100 words) — summarize key takeaways, reinforce main message, include call to action.
5. **Meta description** — under 160 characters, includes primary keyword, compels the click.

### Social Media Post

- **Hook** — first line grabs attention (question, bold statement, number)
- **Body** — 2-4 concise points or a short narrative
- **CTA** — what should the reader do (comment, click, share, tag)
- **Hashtags** — 3-5 relevant hashtags (platform-dependent)

Platform-specific formatting:
- **LinkedIn**: professional but human, paragraph breaks, personal stories, 1,300 chars before "see more"
- **Twitter/X**: concise and punchy, strong opening, threads for longer narratives
- **Instagram**: visual-first, storytelling hooks, line breaks, hashtags at end or first comment
- **Facebook**: conversational, questions drive comments, under 80 chars for link posts

### Email Newsletter

- **Subject line** — under 50 characters, creates curiosity or states clear value
- **Preview text** — complements subject line, does not repeat it
- **Header/hero** — visual anchor and one-line value statement
- **Body sections** — 2-3 content blocks, scannable with bold intro sentences
- **Primary CTA** — one clear action per email, visually distinct
- **Footer** — unsubscribe link, company info, social links

### Landing Page

- **Headline** — primary benefit in under 10 words
- **Subheadline** — elaborates with supporting context
- **Hero section** — headline, subheadline, primary CTA, supporting image/video
- **Value propositions** — 3-4 benefit-driven sections with icons or images
- **Social proof** — testimonials, logos, stats, case study snippets
- **Objection handling** — FAQ or trust signals
- **Final CTA** — repeat primary call to action

### Press Release

- **Headline** — factual, newsworthy, under 80 characters
- **Subheadline** — optional, adds context
- **Dateline** — city, state, date
- **Lead paragraph** — who, what, when, where, why in 2-3 sentences
- **Body paragraphs** — supporting details, quotes, context
- **Boilerplate** — standardized company description
- **Media contact** — name, email, phone

### Case Study

- **Title** — "[Customer] achieves [result] with [product]"
- **Snapshot** — customer name, industry, size, product used, key result
- **Challenge** — what problem the customer faced
- **Solution** — what was implemented and how
- **Results** — quantified outcomes with specific metrics
- **Quote** — customer testimonial
- **CTA** — learn more, get a demo, read more case studies

## Email Sequence Templates

### Onboarding (5-7 emails, 14-21 days)
Welcome → Quick win → Core feature deep dive → Advanced feature → Social proof → Check-in → Upgrade prompt

### Lead Nurture (4-6 emails, 3-4 weeks)
Value-first education → Pain point identification → Solution positioning → Social proof → Soft CTA → Direct CTA

### Re-engagement (3-4 emails, 10-14 days)
"We miss you" → Value reminder → Incentive/exclusive offer → Last chance with deadline

### Win-back (3-5 emails, 30 days)
Friendly check-in → What's new → Special offer → Feedback request → Final goodbye

### Product Launch (4-6 emails, 2-3 weeks)
Teaser → Launch announcement → Feature spotlight → Social proof → Limited-time offer → Reminder

### Educational Drip (5-8 emails, 4-6 weeks)
Introduction → Lesson 1 (foundational) → Lesson 2 (intermediate) → Lesson 3 (advanced) → Practical application → Resource roundup → Graduation

## Writing Best Practices by Channel

### Blog
- Write at 8th-grade reading level for broad audiences; adjust for technical
- Short paragraphs (2-4 sentences)
- Subheadings every 200-300 words
- At least one data point, example, or quote per section
- Active voice, front-load key information

### Email
- Personalize where possible (name, company, behavior)
- One primary CTA per email — visually distinct
- Scannable: bold key phrases, short paragraphs
- Mobile-first design
- Test subject lines, send times, CTA copy, layout

### Web (Landing Pages)
- Lead with benefits, not features
- "You" language — speak directly to the reader
- Minimize jargon unless audience expects it
- Every section answers "so what?" from reader's perspective
- Reduce friction: fewer form fields, clear next steps, trust signals near CTAs

## SEO Fundamentals for Content

### Keyword Strategy
- One primary keyword and 2-3 secondary keywords per piece
- Primary keyword in: headline, first paragraph, one subheading, meta description, URL slug
- Secondary keywords naturally in body copy and subheadings
- Write for humans first — no keyword stuffing

### On-Page SEO Checklist
- Title tag: under 60 characters, includes primary keyword
- Meta description: under 160 characters, includes primary keyword, compels click
- URL slug: short, descriptive, includes primary keyword
- H1: one per page, matches/reflects title tag
- H2/H3: descriptive, include secondary keywords where natural
- Image alt text: descriptive, keyword where relevant
- Internal links: 2-3 links to related content
- External links: 1-2 to authoritative sources

### Content-SEO Integration
- Comprehensive topic coverage (search engines reward depth)
- Answer related questions (check "People Also Ask")
- Update and refresh high-performing content regularly
- Structure for featured snippets: definition paragraphs, numbered lists, tables

## Headline Formulas

- **How to [result] [without obstacle]** — "How to Double Email Open Rates Without Sending More"
- **[Number] [adjective] ways to [result]** — "7 Proven Ways to Reduce Customer Churn"
- **Why [belief] is wrong (and what to do instead)** — "Why More Content Is Not the Answer"
- **The [adjective] guide to [topic]** — "The Complete Guide to B2B Content Marketing"
- **[Do this], not [that]** — "Build a Community, Not Just an Audience"
- **What [result] taught us about [topic]** — "What 10,000 A/B Tests Taught Us About Subject Lines"

## Hook Formulas (Opening Lines)

- **Surprising statistic**: "73% of marketers say their biggest challenge is not budget — it is focus."
- **Contrarian statement**: "The best campaigns start with saying no to most channels."
- **Question**: "When was the last time a marketing email changed what you bought?"
- **Scenario**: "Imagine knowing which messages will land before a campaign goes live."
- **Bold claim**: "Most landing pages lose half their visitors in the first three seconds."
- **Story opening**: "Last quarter, our team spent 20 hours a week on reporting. Here is what we did."

## Call-to-Action Best Practices

### CTA Principles
- Action verbs: "Get", "Start", "Download", "Join", "Try", "See"
- Be specific: "Start your free trial" > "Submit"
- Create genuine urgency: "Join 500 teams already using this"
- Reduce risk: "No credit card required", "Cancel anytime"
- One primary CTA per page or email

### CTA by Context
- **Blog**: "Read our complete guide to [topic]" / "Subscribe for weekly insights"
- **Landing page**: "Start free trial" / "Get a demo" / "See pricing"
- **Email**: "Read the full story" / "Claim your spot" / "Reply and tell us"
- **Social**: "Drop a comment if you agree" / "Save this for later"
- **Case study**: "See how [product] can work for your team"

### CTA Placement
- Above the fold on landing pages
- After establishing value in emails (not first sentence)
- End of blog posts (after earning trust)
- In-line when contextually relevant
- Repeat at bottom of long-form pages
